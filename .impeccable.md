# TravelApp — Impeccable Design Context

## Design Context

### Users
Any group planning a trip together — friend crews, families, couples, mixed groups of any size. They open this app during the fun, anticipatory phase of travel: dreaming, coordinating, budgeting. They're excited, maybe a little overwhelmed, and sharing the screen or link with people who have varying levels of tech comfort. The job: turn trip chaos into a shared, organized plan without killing the vibe.

### Brand Personality
**Fun · Vibrant · Social**

The app should feel like the trip itself is already starting. Energy, color, and personality — not a corporate tool, not a spreadsheet. Think of a great group chat thread that somehow got organized. Tone is warm and optimistic. Nothing feels bureaucratic. Numbers feel exciting (look how much we're doing!) not stressful.

### Aesthetic Direction
- **Primary reference: Notion** — structural clarity, generous whitespace, content-first layout. Information is the design. No chrome for chrome's sake.
- **Layered on top: playful expressiveness** — gradients, emoji, vibrant category colors, personality in micro-copy. Not loud, but alive.
- **Anti-reference: Spreadsheet apps, corporate travel tools** — no dense tables, no gray-on-gray, no enterprise aesthetic.
- **Theme**: Light mode default with dark mode support. Light mode is warm and energetic; dark mode is rich and atmospheric (deep navy, not pure black).
- **Color**: Indigo/violet primary. Category colors are meaningful and consistent (blue = Transport, emerald = Lodging, amber = Food, violet = Activity). Avoid desaturating these.
- **Typography**: Inter. Headings use weight (700–800) for hierarchy, not size alone. Numbers get extra weight and size — they're the star of the show.
- **Shape**: Rounded corners (rounded-2xl standard). Soft, friendly, not harsh.

### Design Principles

1. **Excitement over anxiety** — Trip planning can feel stressful. Every UI choice should reduce friction and amplify the excitement. Countdowns, totals, and progress should feel celebratory, not alarming.

2. **Clarity for everyone** — The app is shared with people who didn't choose it. Labels are plain English. Empty states explain what to do. Nothing requires onboarding.

3. **Social-first information** — Who paid, who's coming, who checked off items — people are the subject, money and logistics are secondary. Put faces (initials, avatars) near data wherever possible.

4. **Delight in the details** — Micro-animations, smooth transitions, emoji, gradient cards. Small surprises make people enjoy using the app. Never skip the transition; never use a flat gray where a gradient fits.

5. **Content hierarchy via weight, not noise** — Follow Notion's lead: whitespace is structure. Use font weight and size to create hierarchy rather than borders, dividers, or background fills. Cards should breathe.
